Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions? We've Got Answers

Everything you need to know about life insurance, explained in plain English.

1. Do I Actually Need Life Insurance? A Simple Guide

Life insurance is crucial if you have anyone who depends on your income—such as a spouse, children, or aging parents. It ensures that if the unexpected happens to you, your loved ones can pay off outstanding debts, cover daily living expenses, and secure their financial future.

2. How Much Life Insurance Coverage Do I Need?

A general rule of thumb is to aim for 10 to 12 times your annual income. However, the exact amount depends on your specific financial obligations, including mortgage debt, personal loans, children's future education costs, and your family's lifestyle needs. You can use our interactive Coverage Calculator above to get a personalized recommendation.

3. What is the Difference Between Term and Permanent Life Insurance?

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period (e.g., 10, 20, or 30 years) and is the most affordable option. Permanent life insurance (like Whole Life or Universal Life) offers lifelong coverage and includes a cash value component that grows over time and can be borrowed against.

5. What Factors Affect My Life Insurance Premiums?

Your premium rate is determined by several factors, including your age, overall health status, family medical history, lifestyle choices (such as smoking), occupation, and the amount of coverage you choose. Securing a policy when you are younger and healthier guarantees the lowest possible rates.

6. How Does Life Insurance Cash Value Work?

An analysis of the "Private Bank" concept within permanent policies. It details how a portion of your premium builds equity over time, which can be accessed for opportunities or emergencies while maintaining protection.

7. Is the Death Benefit Taxable to My Beneficiaries?

Clarifying the tax-free nature of life insurance proceeds. In most cases, the payout is not considered taxable income by the IRS, making it one of the most efficient ways to transfer wealth to the next generation.

8. What Happens When My Term Life Insurance Policy Expires?

Outlining the transition phases. Options include letting the policy expire once debts are cleared, renewing at a new rate, or utilizing a "Conversion Rider" to switch to a permanent policy without a new medical exam.

9. Does Life Insurance Cover Accidental Death?

Confirming that Complete Insurance policies cover a broad range of causes, including accidents and illnesses. It also explains the difference between standard life insurance and "Accidental Death & Dismemberment" (AD&D) riders.

10. Can I Change My Life Insurance Beneficiary Later?

Highlighting the flexibility of "Revocable Beneficiaries." As life evolves through marriage, business partnerships, or new milestones your policy should be updated to reflect your current legacy goals.

11. Is Employer-Provided Life Insurance Enough?

Identifying the "Workplace Trap." While group life insurance is a great perk, it is often tied to your employment and capped at a low multiple of your salary. A private, portable policy ensures you are protected regardless of your job status.
